# Parlor Design Document

# Thread of Fate

*A daily chain of knowledge where every answer is woven into the next. Golden threads reveal hidden connections, ancient looms preserve forgotten destinies, and every correct word strengthens the tapestry of fate. The puzzle has already been written—it is simply waiting to be unraveled.*

---

# Overview

**Thread of Fate** is one of the daily games in the **Parlor** collection (`parlor.an9.dev`).

Thread of Fate is a **trivia chain game**, where **each answer begins with the final letter of the previous answer.** The existing gameplay, puzzle logic, progression, categories, and interface structure should remain exactly as they exist today. This document focuses exclusively on elevating the visual presentation, environmental storytelling, UI design, animations, and overall atmosphere.

Rather than feeling like a sequence of trivia questions, Thread of Fate should feel like uncovering a magical tapestry hidden deep within Parlor's mysterious Victorian estate. Every answer stitches another thread into an ancient weaving that has quietly recorded generations of secrets, conspiracies, murders, hidden passages, and forgotten destinies.

The player isn't simply answering trivia.

They're repairing fate itself.

This should become one of the most elegant and visually sophisticated experiences in the Parlor collection.

The defining emotional moment is:

> *"The final answer is spoken... the last golden thread finds its place... and the complete tapestry reveals the hidden story that fate has been weaving all along."*

# Design Features That Enhance Gameplay

Without changing gameplay:

* The growing tapestry visually represents progress rather than a conventional progress bar.
* Each completed answer permanently stitches itself into the evolving textile, reinforcing the chain mechanic.
* Golden thread subtly previews the connection between consecutive answers without revealing solutions.
* Category transitions are represented by different embroidery styles and weaving techniques rather than generic icons.
* Daily streaks gradually restore the weaving room, adding completed tapestries, repaired furniture, and richer textiles over time.
* Completed games unlock small "Threads of History" cards featuring real historical facts about weaving, embroidery, espionage through textiles, Victorian craftsmanship, secret codes in fabric, or mythological stories surrounding the Fates.
* Archive entries appear as preserved textile collections stored within antique cabinets.

These enhancements create a stronger sense of progression while preserving the existing trivia mechanics.

# Claude Implementation Priorities

1. Preserve the existing Thread of Fate gameplay, trivia chain mechanics, answer logic, layout, and progression exactly as they exist today.
2. Transform the visual identity into a premium Victorian weaving room centered around an ancient loom, handcrafted textiles, embroidery, and living golden threads that embody fate itself.
3. Build reusable architectural systems for cloth rendering, thread animation, loom simulation, embroidered UI components, and environmental storytelling that can extend the broader Parlor design system.
4. Integrate subtle narrative elements from Parlor's Victorian mansion—including mirrors, espionage, hidden messages, family secrets, and murder mysteries—through environmental details rather than gameplay changes.
5. Elevate player feedback through elegant weaving animations, stitched progress, evolving tapestries, premium microinteractions, and atmospheric lighting that reinforce the chain between every answer.
6. Create a cinematic completion ritual where the finished tapestry reveals a hidden truth, making Thread of Fate one of Parlor's visual centerpieces while maintaining exceptional performance across all devices.

Thread of Fate should feel less like answering a sequence of trivia questions and more like uncovering a centuries-old tapestry that has quietly recorded every secret ever hidden within the walls of Parlor Manor. Every answer adds another stitch, every connection strengthens the weave, and by the end of each puzzle, players should feel they have repaired not only a pattern, but a piece of destiny itself.
