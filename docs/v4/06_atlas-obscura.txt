# Parlor Design Document

# Atlas Obscura

*A daily perspective puzzle where constellations only exist from one impossible point of view.*

---

# Overview

**Atlas Obscura** is one of the daily games in the **Parlor** collection (`parlor.an9.dev`).

Each Parlor game explores a different style of logic. Atlas Obscura explores **spatial reasoning**.

Every puzzle consists of a three-dimensional field of stars suspended in space. None of the stars are connected. From nearly every angle they appear to be random. Only from one precise viewpoint do they align into a recognizable constellation.

The player's task is not to identify the constellation.

The task is to discover the one perspective where the constellation exists.

The experience should feel calm, premium, and tactile. There are no timers, scores flying across the screen, or arcade mechanics. Success comes from careful observation and experimentation until the entire sky suddenly "clicks" into place.

The defining emotional moment is:

> "I've been looking at chaos for five minutes... and suddenly it's a swan."

---

# Design Pillars

## Perspective Is The Puzzle

Nothing is hidden.

Nothing rotates.

Nothing changes.

Only the player's viewpoint changes.

The puzzle exists entirely because of perspective.

---

## Elegant Simplicity

The interface disappears.

No flashing UI.

No intrusive hints.

No unnecessary decoration.

The stars are the puzzle.

Everything else exists to support them.

---

## Daily Ritual

Atlas Obscura is intended to be played once per day.

Players open Parlor.

Solve today's constellation.

Share results.

Return tomorrow.

The experience should comfortably fit alongside the other Parlor games as part of a daily routine.

---

## Premium Presentation

Everything should feel expensive.

Smooth camera movement.

High-end lighting.

Subtle sound.

Beautiful transitions.

Nothing should resemble a prototype.

# Gameplay

Every daily puzzle consists of:

* One constellation
* One cloud of stars
* One valid viewing position
* One solution

The player can:

Rotate around the cluster

Zoom

Pan

Inspect from every angle

When the correct viewpoint is reached:

Stars begin glowing

Constellation lines animate

The camera settles naturally

The puzzle completes

# Difficulty Progression

Atlas Obscura follows Parlor's weekly cadence.

Monday introduces approachable puzzles while Sunday presents the most demanding challenge.

## Monday

Large constellation

Few stars

Little depth

Wide solution tolerance

2-3 minute solve

---

## Tuesday

Slightly more overlap

Moderate depth

One misleading alignment

---

## Wednesday

Additional stars

Multiple layers

Perspective becomes important

---

## Thursday

Dense clusters

Foreground and background overlap

Tighter camera precision

---

## Friday

Several convincing false alignments

Players must examine depth carefully

---

## Saturday

Complex constellation

Many decoy stars

Narrow viewing window

---

## Sunday

Master puzzle

Dense stellar field

Multiple fake constellations

Very small camera tolerance

Designed for experienced players

10-20 minute solve

# Camera System

The camera is the primary game mechanic.

It should never feel like OrbitControls.

Movement should resemble a stabilized cinematic camera.

Features include:

Velocity smoothing

Spring interpolation

Micro inertia

Momentum

Soft stopping

No sudden acceleration

No snapping

Every movement should feel physical.

---

# Visual Direction

The player floats in deep interstellar space.

Not science fiction.

Not fantasy.

More like standing inside a planetarium built by a luxury design studio.

The world should feel enormous without distracting from the puzzle.

---

# Background

Procedural sky.

Layered depth.

Extremely subtle movement.

Components include:

Far galaxies

Nebula clouds

Dust fields

Distant stars

Occasional meteors

Slow volumetric gas

The background should remain understated so the playable stars always command attention.

---

# Star Design

Stars are rendered as emissive spheres with physically inspired coloration based on stellar temperature.

Each star contains:

Bright core

Soft halo

Gentle bloom

Very subtle twinkle

Unique brightness variation

Instanced rendering should support hundreds of stars efficiently while maintaining smooth performance.

---

# Constellation Reveal

Before solving:

No visible connections.

No clues.

Only stars.

Upon reaching the solution:

The camera gently settles.

Nearby stars brighten.

Energy traces animate between connected stars.

The completed constellation glows for several seconds before remaining visible as the completion state.

The reveal should feel earned rather than explosive.

---

---

# Hint System

Hints are intentionally restrained.

After several minutes:

A faint shimmer appears within the correct viewing region.

Later:

The acceptable viewing volume becomes subtly more apparent.

Final hint:

A translucent camera silhouette briefly marks the solution before fading.

Hints never reveal the constellation itself.

---

# Claude Implementation Priorities

1. Build the perspective alignment mechanic and deterministic puzzle format first.
2. Create a robust camera system whose movement feels cinematic rather than mechanical.
3. Validate every daily puzzle so there is exactly one satisfying solution and no accidental alternatives.
4. Layer in premium rendering with instanced stars, physically inspired shaders, bloom, and atmospheric depth.
5. Integrate seamlessly with the existing Parlor framework for archives, streaks, statistics, and daily puzzle distribution.
6. Optimize for desktop and mobile, targeting fluid frame rates while maintaining visual quality.

Atlas Obscura should become the most visually striking experience in Parlor, but never at the expense of clarity. Players should leave each session remembering a single magical instant: the moment a meaningless cloud of stars transformed into a constellation simply because they found the right place to stand.
