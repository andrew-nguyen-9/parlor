# Parlor Design Document

# Fractures

*A daily test of knowledge where fractured reflections reveal hidden truths. Every answer restores clarity. Every shard tells part of the story. Only by piecing together knowledge can the mirror become whole again.*

---

# Overview

**Fractures** is one of the daily games in the **Parlor** collection (`parlor.an9.dev`).

Fractures is a **category-based trivia game**, inspired by the approachable format of Trivia Crack. **The existing gameplay, question flow, categories, scoring, and layout should remain exactly as they are today.** This document focuses exclusively on elevating the visual identity, UI, atmosphere, animations, and overall presentation.

Rather than feeling like a generic trivia app, Fractures should feel like interacting with an ancient mirror that has been shattered into countless pieces. Every correct answer slowly restores order while every incorrect answer leaves new cracks spreading across the surface. The interface should constantly remind players that knowledge repairs what has been broken.

This should become one of the most visually striking games in the Parlor collection.

The defining emotional moment is:

> *"The final question is answered... the fractured mirror finally reforms... and one perfect reflection stares back."*

## Elegant, Not Destructive

Avoid making the game feel violent or chaotic.

The inspiration is antique mirrors, fractured crystal, and old glass—not explosions or broken windows.

Think:

Luxury hotel mirror.

Victorian mansion.

Abandoned ballroom.

Moonlight reflecting across cracked glass.

The mood should feel mysterious and premium.

---

## Premium Presentation

Every interaction should feel polished.

Glass bends light.

Smoke moves naturally.

Lighting changes with progress.

Nothing should resemble a standard mobile trivia interface.

# Design Features That Enhance Gameplay

Without changing gameplay itself:

* Mirror becomes progressively clearer as players answer correctly.
* Completed categories restore larger portions of the reflection.
* Daily streaks subtly repair permanent decorative elements surrounding the mirror frame.
* Archive entries appear inside a gallery of restored mirrors.
* Category selection gently illuminates matching fracture paths.
* The current score is reflected naturally inside the mirror rather than appearing as a disconnected HUD element.
* End-of-game statistics animate across repaired glass surfaces rather than standard cards.

These features strengthen visual progression while preserving the existing trivia mechanics.

# Claude Implementation Priorities

1. Preserve the existing Fractures gameplay, trivia flow, categories, question system, layout, and scoring exactly as they exist today.
2. Redesign the visual identity around antique broken mirrors, fractured glass, smoked crystal, elegant reflections, and premium atmospheric lighting.
3. Introduce layered environmental effects including drifting smoke, floating glass particles, dynamic reflections, subtle refraction, and evolving fracture animations that reinforce progress without distracting from gameplay.
4. Extend the existing Parlor design system with premium glass-inspired UI components, silver detailing, refined typography, cinematic transitions, and luxurious materials instead of generic trivia interfaces.
5. Elevate gameplay feedback through elegant glass restoration animations, progressive mirror reconstruction, refined audio cues, and satisfying microinteractions that visually reward correct answers while maintaining the existing mechanics.
6. Build reusable systems for reflections, fracture masks, smoke, lighting, and glass materials so Fractures becomes one of Parlor's flagship visual experiences while remaining performant across desktop and mobile.

Fractures should become a trivia experience unlike any other. Players shouldn't remember answering questions inside a standard interface—they should remember slowly restoring an impossibly beautiful shattered mirror, watching every piece return to its rightful place through knowledge alone.
