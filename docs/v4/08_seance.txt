# Parlor Design Document

# Séance

*A daily ritual of deduction where whispers from beyond, flickering candlelight, and restless spirits reveal hidden truths. Every clue is communicated through supernatural signs. Every revelation follows pure logic. Only by conducting the séance correctly can the spirits finally speak.*

---

# Overview

**Séance** is one of the daily games in the **Parlor** collection (`parlor.an9.dev`).

Séance is a classic **logic grid puzzle**, and **the gameplay should remain exactly as it exists today.** This document focuses exclusively on elevating the visual presentation, atmosphere, UI polish, animations, and immersive interactions.

Rather than feeling like a spreadsheet wrapped in a spooky theme, Séance should feel like participating in an authentic Victorian séance inside an old haunted parlor. The player is seated around an antique Ouija board, candles burn low, unseen spirits react to every deduction, and the room itself becomes more alive as the puzzle progresses.

This should become one of Parlor's flagship visual experiences—premium, elegant, mysterious, and deeply atmospheric.

The defining emotional moment is:

> *"The final deduction is made... the room falls silent... the planchette glides on its own... and the spirits finally reveal themselves."*

---

# Design Pillars

## Logic Never Changes

The existing puzzle mechanics are excellent.

Nothing about the puzzle flow, grid layout, clue system, or solving experience should change.

Everything proposed here enhances presentation—not gameplay.

---

They simply make the world feel alive.

Examples include:

A transparent child briefly peeks around the edge of the screen.

Shadow figures drift behind candles.

Small glowing orbs float across the room.

# The Planchette

The planchette becomes the game's signature animated element.

Very subtle movement.

Tiny rotations.

Barely perceptible drifting.

Hints:

# Design Features That Enhance Gameplay

Without changing the puzzle itself:

* Candles brighten as certainty increases.
* The room subtly becomes less haunted as the player approaches the solution.
* Ghosts react more frequently when players make correct deductions.
* Solved relationships receive elegant engraved embellishments rather than simple highlights.
* Daily completion unlocks a "Spirit Memory" card featuring a historical séance fact, Victorian spiritualism anecdote, folklore, or supernatural mythology.
* Archive entries appear as pages inside an old medium's journal.
* Streak milestones subtly restore additional decorations to the séance room over time.

These additions create a stronger sense of progression while preserving the existing puzzle structure.

# Claude Implementation Priorities

1. Preserve the existing Séance gameplay, logic grid mechanics, layout, and puzzle structure exactly as they are today.
2. Transform the visual identity into a premium Victorian séance experience inspired by authentic Ouija boards, antique spiritualism, and haunted parlors.
3. Introduce rich ambient animations including candlelight, smoke, drifting dust, ghost interactions, moving shadows, and subtle environmental storytelling that never distracts from solving.
4. Redesign every interface element using the existing Parlor design system, adding carved wood, brass, parchment, engraved details, elegant typography, and cinematic lighting instead of generic game UI.
5. Elevate player feedback through atmospheric microinteractions, a living planchette, spirit reactions, and a memorable end-of-puzzle ritual that celebrates completion without altering gameplay.
6. Build reusable visual systems for spectral effects, ambient lighting, particles, and haunted room animations so Séance stands alongside Parlor's flagship experiences while remaining performant across devices.

Séance should become one of the defining visual experiences in Parlor. Players shouldn't remember flashy effects—they should remember the feeling of sitting alone in a candlelit room, making one final deduction, and watching the spirits answer because their logic was flawless.
