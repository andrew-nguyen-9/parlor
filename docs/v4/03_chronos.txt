# Parlor Design Document

# Chronos

*A daily mechanical logic puzzle where every gear has a purpose, every component obeys real constraints, and only one perfect assembly can bring time back to life.*

---

# Overview

**Chronos** is one of the daily games in the **Parlor** collection (`parlor.an9.dev`).

Each Parlor game explores a different style of logic. Chronos explores **mechanical reasoning**.

Every daily puzzle presents the player with an intricate mechanical watch movement that has been partially disassembled. Gears, bridges, screws, escapements, springs, and precision components have been misplaced, removed, or installed incorrectly.

The player's objective is not simply to rebuild the watch.

The objective is to understand the logic of the mechanism and restore it until the movement comes alive.

Every placement matters.

Every gear changes the entire system.

Every mistake has a visible mechanical consequence.

The experience should feel calm, premium, and deeply tactile. There are no timers counting down, no frantic mechanics, and no arbitrary guessing. Players succeed through observation, deduction, and experimentation until dozens of independent mechanical systems suddenly begin working together.

The defining emotional moment is:

> "Everything finally fits... and suddenly the entire watch begins ticking."

# Gameplay

Every daily puzzle consists of:

* One watch movement
* One collection of mechanical components
* One logical solution
* One successful simulation

The player can:

Rotate the movement

Zoom

Inspect components

Move gears

Install bridges

Place screws

Adjust escapements

Wind springs

Run the simulation

If the mechanism is assembled correctly:

Power begins flowing.

The gear train rotates.

The escapement locks and unlocks rhythmically.

The balance wheel oscillates.

The second hand begins ticking.

The puzzle completes.

# Weekly Difficulty Progression

Chronos follows Parlor's weekly cadence.

Monday introduces approachable puzzles while Sunday presents the most intricate movement of the week.

## Monday

One primary mechanic.

Few components.

Wide placement tolerance.

Simple gear train.

3 to 5 minute solve.

---

## Tuesday

Additional gears.

Simple bridge placement.

One misleading configuration.

---

## Wednesday

Multiple interconnected systems.

Power flow becomes important.

Players begin reasoning about gear direction.

---

## Thursday

Layered construction.

Multiple bridges.

Vertical relationships.

Timing begins to matter.

---

## Friday

Gear ratios.

Escapement tuning.

Precision alignment.

False mechanical solutions.

---

## Saturday

Complications.

Several subsystems.

Power must reach multiple mechanisms simultaneously.

Numerous convincing dead ends.

---

## Sunday

Masterpiece puzzle.

Full watch movement.

Complex interactions.

Hidden dependencies.

Every mechanic introduced throughout the week appears together.

Designed for experienced players.

15 to 20 minute solve.

# Hint System

Hints remain intentionally subtle.

After several minutes:

A faint highlight appears around one relevant region of the movement.

Later:

Potential connection points become softly illuminated.

Final hint:

A translucent outline briefly shows the correct position for one component before fading.

Hints never solve multiple steps at once.

# Claude Implementation Priorities

1. Build a deterministic mechanical simulation engine before creating content.
2. Develop a modular component system where every gear, bridge, screw, spring, and escapement follows reusable physical rules.
3. Create a robust constraint solver that guarantees every daily puzzle has exactly one logical solution.
4. Implement premium Three.js rendering with physically based materials, cinematic camera movement, depth of field, selective bloom, and highly polished animations.
5. Design a procedural daily puzzle generator that scales naturally from Monday's introductory mechanisms to Sunday's full masterwork movements while maintaining fairness.
6. Integrate seamlessly with the existing Parlor framework for archives, streaks, statistics, collections, and daily puzzle distribution.
7. Optimize for desktop and tablet, targeting a stable 60 FPS while preserving visual fidelity and mechanical responsiveness.

Chronos should become the flagship craftsmanship experience within Parlor. Players should finish each session remembering one quiet, satisfying moment: the instant an inert collection of brass and steel transforms into a living machine, ticking steadily because every piece is finally in exactly the right place.
